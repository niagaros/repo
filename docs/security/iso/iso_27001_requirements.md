# ISO 27001 Certification Requirements

## 1. Establish the ISMS (Information Security Management System)
- Define the **scope** of the ISMS (what part of the business is covered).
- Identify stakeholders and their expectations.
- Create an **Information Security Policy**.

## 2. Conduct Risk Assessment & Risk Treatment
- Identify information security risks.
- Assess impact and likelihood.
- Decide on risk treatment (avoid, reduce, transfer, accept).
- Create a **Risk Treatment Plan (RTP)**.
- Maintain a **Risk Register**.

## 3. Define and Document Mandatory ISO 27001 Documents
You must create, implement, and maintain documentation including:
- Information Security Policy
- Scope Document
- Risk Assessment Methodology
- Statement of Applicability (SoA)
- Risk Treatment Plan
- Internal Audit Procedure
- Corrective Action Procedure

## 4. Implement Required Annex A Security Controls
Examples (from Annex A of ISO 27001:2022):
- Access Control Policies
- Asset Inventory
- Password & Authentication Standards
- Backup & Recovery Policies
- Logging and Monitoring
- Supplier / Third-Party Management
- Business Continuity Planning
- Incident Management Process

> Note: You implement only the controls relevant for your risks.

## 5. Training and Awareness
- All employees must be trained in security policies.
- Keep records of training and attendance.

## 6. Monitor, Measure, and Improve
- Conduct **Internal Audits**.
- Perform **Management Review** meetings.
- Track incidents and improvements (PDCA cycle: Plan → Do → Check → Act).

## 7. External ISO 27001 Audit
### Stage 1 Audit – Documentation Review
Auditor checks if documentation meets ISO requirements.

### Stage 2 Audit – Implementation & Effectiveness
Auditor checks if the security controls are implemented and working.

### Certification Granted (valid for 3 years)
- Annual surveillance audits required.
- Continuous improvement must be demonstrated.
