# Internal Audit Procedure & Checklist

---

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of the Internal Audit is to verify whether processes comply with:

- Internal policies and procedures
- Applicable regulations or standards (ISO, SOC 2, internal controls, etc.)
- Expected performance and effectiveness

Internal Audits provide objective evaluation and help identify opportunities for improvement.

---

## 2. Scope
This applies to:

- All processes included in the management system
- All departments and personnel participating in those processes
- Any system, document, or activity subject to audit

---

## 3. Audit Objectives
Internal audits must determine whether:

| Objective | Definition |
|----------|-------------|
| **Conformance** | Are we doing what we say we do? |
| **Effectiveness** | Is the process working as intended? |
| **Improvement** | Can we do it better? |

---

## 4. Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| **Audit Manager / Lead Auditor** | Plans and executes the audit, reports findings |
| **Auditor(s)** | Collect evidence, evaluate compliance |
| **Process Owner / Auditee** | Provides requested documents, responds to findings |
| **Management** | Reviews results, assigns corrective actions |

Auditors must be **independent of the process being audited**.

---

## Internal Audit Checklist

### A. Audit Planning

| Step | Task | Owner | Status |
|------|------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Define audit scope (process, department, standard) |  |
| 2 | Define audit objectives and criteria |  |
| 3 | Assign an independent auditor |  |
| 4 | Schedule the audit and notify auditees |  |
| 5 | Prepare audit plan and checklist |  |
| 6 | Collect applicable documents (policies, logs, records) |  |

---

### B. Audit Execution

| Step | Task | Owner | Status |
|------|------|--------|--------|
| 7 | Conduct opening meeting |  |
| 8 | Perform interviews with key personnel |  |
| 9 | Review documents and evidence |  |
|10 | Observe processes and activities |  |
|11 | Identify nonconformities, risks, and improvement areas |  |
|12 | Conduct closing meeting and present preliminary findings |  |

---

### C. Reporting

| Step | Task | Owner | Status |
|------|------|--------|--------|
|13 | Document findings and evidence |  |
|14 | Categorize results (Conformity / Minor NC / Major NC / Opportunity for Improvement) |  |
|15 | Issue final audit report to management |  |

---

### D. Corrective Action & Follow-up

| Step | Task | Owner | Status |
|------|------|--------|--------|
|16 | Assign corrective actions with deadlines and owners |  |
|17 | Verify effectiveness of corrective actions |  |
|18 | Close audit and archive records |  |

---

## 5. Audit Evidence
Evidence must be:

- Objective
- Verifiable
- Traceable to records or observations

Examples: screenshots, logs, reports, interviews, tickets.

---

## 6. Nonconformity Categories

| Category | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Major NC** | Systemic issue or failure to meet critical requirements |
| **Minor NC** | Isolated issue not affecting performance or security |
| **Opportunity for Improvement (OFI)** | No failure, but improvement is recommended |

---

## 7. Required Records

- Audit plan
- Audit checklist
- Audit report
- Corrective action tracking
