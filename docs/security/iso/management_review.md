# Management Review

---

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of the Management Review is to ensure that the organization’s management system is effective, aligned with strategic objectives, and continually improving.  
Management Review evaluates:

- Performance of key processes
- Compliance with internal/external requirements
- Risks, issues, and improvement opportunities

Management Review is not operational problem-solving—it is strategic assessment and decision-making.

---

## 2. Scope
This review applies to the full management system, including:

- Quality / IT / Security / Operational processes
- Relevant standards or frameworks (ex: ISO 9001, ISO 27001, internal governance practices)

---

## 3. Frequency
Management Reviews must be held at least **annually**, but may occur quarterly or after major changes.

---

## 4. Required Inputs (What must be reviewed)

| Input Area | Examples |
|------------|----------|
| **Status of previous actions** | Action item closure, unresolved issues |
| **Performance and KPIs** | Metrics, dashboards, process effectiveness |
| **Nonconformities and corrective actions** | Audit findings, customer complaints |
| **Risk and opportunity assessment** | New risks, mitigations, improvements |
| **Resource adequacy** | Staffing, tools, training, budget |
| **Customer and stakeholder feedback** | Satisfaction, escalations |
| **Changes that could affect the system** | Organizational change, new regulation |

---

## 5. Outputs (What must be decided)

The outcome of the Management Review must include decisions on:

- Changes or improvements to the management system
- Action items with owners and timelines
- Resource needs (budget, tools, training)
- Updates to objectives, risks, or policies

---

## 6. Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| **Top Management / Executive Sponsor** | Ensures review is completed and decisions are implemented |
| **Process Owners / Department Leads** | Provide input data (metrics, issues, improvements) |
| **Management Review Facilitator** | Organizes meeting, consolidates report, tracks actions |

---

## 7. Meeting Overview (example structure)

1. Review of previous action items
2. System performance and KPIs
3. Risks, issues, nonconformities
4. Improvement opportunities
5. Decisions, actions, resource needs

---

## 8. Records & Evidence
The following artifacts must be retained:

- Agenda
- Meeting minutes
- Action log
- Approvals
